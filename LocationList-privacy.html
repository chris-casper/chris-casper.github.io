LocationList

Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 30, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the LocationList Android app (package name im.casper.locationlist, the “App”) handles your information. The App is developed by Chris Casper (the “Developer”, “we”, “us”).

The short version. LocationList keeps your data on your own device.

  • Your saved locations, notes, tags, and photos are stored locally on your device. We do not collect them, receive them, or have access to them.
  • The App has no user accounts, shows no ads, and includes no third-party analytics or tracking software.
  • Some optional features connect to the internet (OpenStreetMap map tiles and, if you set it up, an RF Sim server). Those are described below.

Information the App handles

Location content you create. Names, latitude/longitude, descriptions, notes, groups, tags, and photos that you add to your saved locations. This is stored only in the App’s local database on your device.

Photos. When you attach a photo using the camera or pick one from your gallery, a copy is saved in the App’s private storage on your device.

Device location (GPS). When you tap “Use current location”, the App reads your device’s current location to fill in coordinates. This happens only when you request it, only while the App is in use (no background location), and the coordinate is stored only with the location you are creating. It is not sent to the Developer.

Map imagery. To display maps, the App requests map tiles from the basemap provider you select in Settings (for example OpenStreetMap, OpenTopoMap, USGS, or Humanitarian OpenStreetMap). When your device requests these tiles, the provider receives standard network request information (such as the map area being viewed and your device’s IP address). This information is handled under each provider’s own privacy policy, not ours.

RF coverage / Viewshed features (optional). If you choose to use the RF-Sim and Viewshed features, you must first enter a server address and API key in Settings. When you submit a request, the App sends the coordinates and parameters you selected, along with your API key, to the server you configured, and downloads the results it returns. This data is transmitted to and handled by the operator of that server. If you do not configure and use these features, no such data is sent.

Sharing and export. When you use the App’s share or export options (for example sharing a KML/KMZ file, coordinates, or a photo), the data you choose to share is sent to the destination you pick in your device’s share menu. You control what is shared and where it goes.

How the information is used

Information is used only to provide the App’s features: saving and organizing your locations, showing them on a map, filling in coordinates, attaching photos, and—if you enable it—submitting RF coverage requests to your configured server. We do not use your information for advertising or profiling, and we do not collect or sell it.

Permissions

The App requests the following Android permissions:

  • Location (precise) — to fill in coordinates when you ask for your current location. Used only while the App is in use.
  • Camera — to take photos to attach to a location.
  • Photos / media — to attach existing images from your device.
  • Internet / network access — to download map tiles and, if configured, to communicate with your RF coverage server.

You can deny or revoke any of these in your device settings; features that depend on them will be unavailable.

Data storage, retention, and deletion

Your saved locations and photos remain on your device until you delete them. You can delete an individual location in the App, clear all app data from the Settings “danger zone”, or uninstall the App to remove its local data. Because this data lives on your device, deleting it is fully in your control.

Data you send to a third-party map provider or to your own RF coverage server is retained according to that party’s policies, not ours.

Security

The App stores your data in its private, app-specific storage on your device. Network requests to map and coverage servers use whatever transport you configure; note that connecting to a server over plain HTTP (for example a local network address) is not encrypted in transit. HTTP should be only for testing. HTTPS is highly encouraged.

Children’s privacy

LocationList is not directed to children and does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted at this location with an updated “Last updated” date. Continued use of the App after a change means you accept the updated policy.
